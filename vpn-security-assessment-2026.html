Enterprise VPN Security Assessment: Q1 2026 Comparative Analysis

Michael Chen, Senior Security Analyst · March 31, 2026 · 14 min read

As enterprise perimeter security continues to evolve, VPN appliances remain a critical component of remote access architecture for most large organizations. This quarterly assessment evaluates the security posture of the four leading enterprise VPN vendors based on vulnerability disclosure data, patch response times, deployment telemetry from Meridian’s managed services client base, and independent penetration testing conducted by our assessment team during Q1 2026.

All testing was conducted under standard engagement terms with vendor awareness. Findings were shared with each vendor’s PSIRT team prior to publication per Meridian’s coordinated disclosure policy.

Vendor Comparative Overview

The four vendors assessed in this report represent approximately 81% of the enterprise VPN market by deployment volume, according to IDC’s Q4 2025 Network Security Tracker. Each vendor was evaluated across five categories: vulnerability response time, patch adoption rate, default configuration security, authentication framework strength, and logging/audit capability.

Vulnerability Metrics

We tracked all CVEs published against these products between October 2025 and March 2026, filtering for vulnerabilities with a CVSS base score of 7.0 or higher (High and Critical severity).

Vendor Critical CVEs High CVEs Avg. Patch Time Fastest Patch
Cisco 2 5 8.3 days 3 days
Palo Alto Networks 3 4 11.7 days 5 days
Fortinet 1 6 6.2 days 2 days
Ivanti 4 7 18.4 days 9 days

Ivanti continues to lag significantly behind competitors in patch response time. The 18.4-day average for Ivanti compares unfavorably to the industry benchmark of 7–10 days established by the Ponemon Institute’s 2025 Vulnerability Response Report. Ivanti’s longest patch cycle during this period was 34 days for CVE-2025-22457, a critical pre-authentication buffer overflow — a delay that drew significant criticism from the security community.

Patch Adoption Rates

Using telemetry from Meridian’s managed security services client base (approximately 2,400 enterprise endpoints across 340 organizations), we measured the percentage of appliances running the latest patched firmware within 30 days of each critical patch release.

Vendor 30-Day Adoption Rate 90-Day Adoption Rate Still Unpatched (as of March 31)
Cisco 62% 89% 11%
Palo Alto Networks 54% 83% 17%
Fortinet 68% 91% 9%
Ivanti 41% 72% 28%

The low adoption rates for Ivanti are particularly concerning given the product’s history of actively exploited vulnerabilities. Of the 28% of Ivanti Connect Secure appliances in our dataset that remain unpatched, Meridian identified 14 instances where indicators of compromise consistent with known exploitation were present in appliance logs — suggesting active threat actor access to unpatched devices.

Key Findings

1. Ivanti Connect Secure Remains the Highest-Risk Product

For the third consecutive quarter, Ivanti Connect Secure received the lowest overall score in our assessment. The combination of a high CVE count (4 critical, 7 high), slow patch response (18.4-day average), low patch adoption (41% at 30 days), and confirmed active exploitation makes this product the highest-risk VPN platform currently deployed in enterprise environments.

Meridian recommends that organizations currently running Ivanti Connect Secure begin evaluating migration to alternative platforms. For organizations that must continue operating Ivanti infrastructure, we recommend implementing the compensating controls outlined in our companion technical brief (MSR-2026-VPN-TB-004).

2. Authentication Bypass Patterns Are Emerging Across Vendors

A concerning pattern observed across multiple vendors this quarter is the emergence of pre-authentication vulnerability classes — flaws that can be exploited without valid credentials. Three of the four vendors disclosed at least one pre-authentication vulnerability during the assessment period. This trend correlates with broader industry data from Verizon’s 2026 DBIR, which found that 29% of enterprise network breaches in 2025 involved exploitation of VPN or remote access infrastructure as the initial access vector — a figure that has increased steadily from 22% in 2023.

3. Fortinet Leads in Patch Velocity

Fortinet demonstrated the fastest average patch response time (6.2 days) and the highest 30-day patch adoption rate (68%) of any vendor in our assessment. This improvement is largely attributed to Fortinet’s investment in automated patch distribution via FortiManager, which reduces the operational burden on customer IT teams and accelerates deployment cycles. Cisco performed comparably at 8.3 days.

4. Default Configuration Weaknesses Persist

During penetration testing of default and common configurations, Meridian identified security-relevant misconfigurations in 73% of the deployments assessed. The most common issues were:

  • Weak or missing multi-factor authentication enforcement (present in 44% of deployments)
  • Default TLS cipher suite configurations that include deprecated algorithms (38%)
  • Insufficient session timeout values exceeding 24 hours (31%)
  • Lack of split-tunnel restrictions allowing full network access from remote endpoints (27%)

These misconfiguration rates have remained largely unchanged since our Q3 2025 assessment, suggesting that vendor default settings and customer security hygiene have not materially improved despite increased public attention to VPN security following the high-profile Ivanti campaigns of 2024.

5. Palo Alto GlobalProtect: Certificate Validation Gap

During testing of Palo Alto Networks GlobalProtect 6.2.x, Meridian discovered a certificate validation inconsistency (tracked internally as MSR-VULN-2026-GP-017) that could allow an attacker on the same network segment to intercept and downgrade a GlobalProtect client connection under specific conditions. The issue requires the target user to be connecting from a network where the attacker can perform ARP spoofing, and only affects configurations using the HIP-based auto-connect profile.

Meridian reported this finding to Palo Alto Networks PSIRT on February 14, 2026, and received acknowledgment on February 16. Palo Alto Networks has classified the issue as medium severity and indicated a fix will be included in GlobalProtect 6.2.5, scheduled for release in Q2 2026. Meridian concurs with the medium severity assessment given the local network access requirement.

Deployment Recommendations

Based on this quarter’s assessment, Meridian recommends the following for enterprise security teams:

  1. Prioritize Ivanti migration planning. Organizations with more than 50 Ivanti Connect Secure appliances should initiate formal evaluation of alternative platforms within the next 90 days, with a target migration completion of Q4 2026.
  2. Enforce MFA on all VPN access without exception. The 44% MFA gap identified in our assessment represents a critical exposure that is trivially exploitable.
  3. Implement automated patch management. Organizations using Fortinet’s FortiManager or Cisco’s SecureX platform should enable auto-deployment for critical patches to reduce the 30-day adoption gap.
  4. Review TLS cipher configurations. Deprecate TLS 1.0/1.1 and remove CBC-mode ciphers from all VPN endpoint configurations.
  5. Monitor for Ivanti IOCs. Organizations still running Ivanti Connect Secure should immediately implement the detection signatures published in CISA Advisory AA24-060A and Meridian’s companion IOC feed.
Methodology Note: This assessment is based on data from Meridian’s managed services client base, independent penetration testing, and publicly available vulnerability disclosures. Vendor scores reflect a weighted composite of the five evaluation categories. Individual organizational risk may vary based on deployment configuration, network architecture, and compensating controls. Detailed scoring methodology is available in our companion document MSR-2026-VPN-METH-001.
